The Historical Record

They Were Removed

One hundred angels named in the oldest texts of human history. Named, documented, venerated. Then struck from the record. Not lost. Not disproven. Deliberately removed.

Council of Rome · 382 AD

↓ Read the record
Before

The earliest civilisations named them.

The Book of Enoch. 3rd century BC. One hundred angels. Named. Documented. Scripture.

They were not mythology. Not metaphor. They were as real as time and stone.

The Decision

382 AD. A council of men decided what God was allowed to say.

325 AD

Council of Nicaea

The first cuts. One empire. One doctrine.

364 AD

Council of Laodicea

Canon 35. Angel veneration condemned. Declared heresy.

382 AD

Council of Rome

The canon is set. Of one hundred named angels — two survive. Michael. Gabriel. The rest are removed.

397 AD

Council of Carthage

The canon is closed. Their names go unspoken in any church for over a thousand years.

What Was Removed

Ninety-eight names. Struck from the record.

Uriel  ·  Saraqael  ·  Raguel  ·  Remiel  ·  Azazel  ·  Penemue  ·  Kokabiel  ·  Shamsiel  ·  Sariel  ·  Baraqel  ·  Tamiel  ·  Hananel  ·  and eighty-eight more.

Officially Removed · 382 AD

And there were one hundred, each known by name.

Why

They wanted God to be feared.

They made spirituality feel accessible — not feared. Not idolised. Simply present. That had to go.

What Survived

Everything.

The Ethiopian Orthodox Church never adopted the Roman canon. The Book of Enoch never left their scripture.

In 1947, a shepherd threw a stone into a cave above the Dead Sea and heard something break. Two thousand years. The names were intact.

Dead Poet remembers.

One hundred garments. One hundred Soul records. Each named for an angel that was removed. Permanent. Unchained. Unreachable by any council.
